Cataract Risk-Based Clinical and Lifestyle Recommendations

------------------------------------------------------------

LOW RISK (Early / Mild Condition):
- No immediate medical intervention required.
- Maintain regular eye check-ups (every 6–12 months).
- Follow a balanced diet rich in antioxidants (Vitamin A, C, E).
- Reduce prolonged screen exposure and take frequent breaks.
- Wear UV-protective sunglasses outdoors.
- Maintain good hydration and healthy sleep habits.

Lifestyle Advice:
- Increase intake of leafy vegetables (spinach, kale).
- Include fruits like carrots, oranges, and berries.
- Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption.

------------------------------------------------------------

MEDIUM RISK (Moderate Condition):
- Regular ophthalmologist monitoring is advised.
- Early signs of visual impairment may be present.
- Follow strict dietary and lifestyle modifications.
- Limit night driving and activities requiring sharp vision.
- Monitor symptoms like blurred vision, glare, or sensitivity to light.

Lifestyle Advice:
- Increase antioxidant-rich foods and omega-3 intake.
- Manage blood sugar levels (especially for diabetic patients).
- Reduce screen time and eye strain.
- Use proper lighting while reading or working.

Clinical Recommendation:
- Periodic eye examinations (every 3–6 months).
- Consider medical consultation if symptoms worsen.

------------------------------------------------------------

HIGH RISK (Severe Condition):
- Immediate consultation with an ophthalmologist is strongly recommended.
- High probability of significant visual impairment.
- Surgical evaluation (cataract surgery) may be required.
- Avoid driving, especially at night or in low visibility conditions.
- Strict adherence to medical advice is necessary.

Lifestyle Advice:
- Follow a nutrient-rich diet with high antioxidant content.
- Avoid exposure to UV light and environmental pollutants.
- Use protective eyewear consistently.
- Maintain strict control of diabetes or other comorbid conditions.

Clinical Recommendation:
- Detailed eye examination and imaging required.
- Evaluate for surgical intervention.
- Post-operative care planning may be necessary.

------------------------------------------------------------

GENERAL PREVENTION GUIDELINES:
- Protect eyes from UV radiation using sunglasses.
- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.
- Maintain a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
- Control chronic diseases such as diabetes and hypertension.
- Ensure regular eye screening, especially for people above 40 years.
